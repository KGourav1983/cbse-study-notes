# 📘 Chapter 1: Natural Resources and Their Use
### Quick Study Notes — Grade 8

---

## 🎯 The Big Questions
1. How do we categorise natural resources?
2. How is the distribution of natural resources connected to life?
3. What happens when we over-exploit natural resources?

---

## 1️⃣ When Does Nature Become a "Resource"?

**Nature** = all life and non-life things around us that humans didn't create.

Nature becomes a **resource** only when:
- ✅ We can **technologically** access it
- ✅ It is **economically feasible** to extract/use it
- ✅ It is **culturally acceptable** to use it

> 💡 **Example:** Petroleum deep under the ocean isn't a "resource" until we have the tech and money to extract it. Trees in a sacred grove may never become a "resource" because cutting them is culturally unacceptable.

**Word Alert:** "Exploitation" here just means *extraction, use, and consumption* — not something negative!

---

## 2️⃣ Two Ways to Categorise Natural Resources

### 🅰️ Based on USE

| Category | What it means | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Essential for life** | Can't survive without these | Air, water, soil |
| **For materials** | Used to make objects | Wood → furniture, marble, gold |
| **For energy** | Powers our world | Coal, sunlight, wind, water |

### 🅱️ Based on RENEWABILITY

**🌿 Renewable Resources**
- Nature restores & regenerates them *if used sustainably*
- Examples: sunlight, wind, flowing water, forest timber
- ⚠️ **Condition:** Don't use faster than nature can replace it!

**⛏️ Non-Renewable Resources**
- Take millions of years to form; can't be replaced at the rate we use them
- Examples: coal, petroleum, iron, copper, gold
- 🇮🇳 India's coal reserves may last only ~50 more years!

> **Key Idea:** Nature works in *cycles* with zero waste (a fallen tree decomposes → enriches soil → new trees grow). Human pollution disrupts this cycle.

---

## 3️⃣ Ecosystem Functions vs. Ecosystem Services

- **Ecosystem Function** = What nature naturally does (a forest filters water, prevents soil erosion)
- **Ecosystem Service** = The *benefit* humans get from that function (we get clean water!)

🌳 Fun Fact: A mature tree makes ~275 litres of oxygen/day. A human needs ~350 litres/day!

---

## 4️⃣ Distribution of Resources & Its Effects

Natural resources are **NOT evenly spread** across India or the world. This leads to:

- 🏭 Industries & townships growing near resource-rich areas → jobs & development
- 🏚️ BUT also displacement of local people from their homes
- ⚔️ Conflicts over shared resources (e.g., **Kaveri river water dispute** between Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Kerala & Puducherry)
- 🌍 Wars fought globally over resource control

---

## 5️⃣ The "Natural Resource Curse" 🪄

**Surprising fact:** Countries rich in resources aren't always rich in wealth!

**Why?** They often fail to build industries that turn raw resources into higher-value products.

✅ **India has largely avoided this** by investing in industries and skills to use its resources wisely.

---

## 6️⃣ Real-Life Case Studies

### 😟 Punjab — Overexploitation of Groundwater
- 1960s Green Revolution → high-yield crops needed more water
- Free electricity → farmers over-pumped groundwater
- **Result:** Groundwater now inaccessible below 30 metres in large areas; ~80% of Punjab is "over-exploited"
- Short-term food security ✅ but long-term sustainability crisis ❌

### 😊 Sikkim — Organic Farming Success
- Farmers shifted away from costly chemical farming
- Initial yield drop → but after 5 years, farms thrived
- **2016:** Sikkim became India's first **100% organic state**
- Result: biodiversity returned, tourism grew, farmer incomes rose by 20%!

---

## 7️⃣ Moving Toward Sustainability

| Problem | Traditional/Modern Solution |
|---|---|
| Soil degradation from chemicals | Cow dung fertiliser, mulching, multi-cropping |
| Polluting cement industry | Mud/stone construction, plant-based & recycled materials |
| Fossil fuel dependence | Solar & wind energy (India-France **International Solar Alliance**, 2015) |

🔆 India's solar parks (like in Rajasthan) can power a huge share of local electricity needs!

---

## ✅ Before You Move On — Key Takeaways

1. **Natural resources** = materials from Nature that are valuable to humans
2. Resources can be **renewable** or **non-renewable**
3. The **"resource curse"** can be beaten with technology & skill development
4. We must use renewables *carefully* and non-renewables *judiciously* — this is called **stewardship**
