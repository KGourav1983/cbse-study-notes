## Answer Key

1. **Franchise** is a right granted by the constitution or government — specifically, the right to vote. It is also known as **suffrage**.

2. **NOTA** stands for **None Of The Above** — an option that allows voters to reject all contesting candidates.

3. The Election Commission of India was established in **1950**.

4. **State Election Commissions** are responsible for conducting elections to gram panchayats and urban local bodies.

5. Under **First-Past-the-Post**, the candidate who receives the **most votes** in a constituency is declared the winner, even if they do not secure 50% or more of the total votes cast.

6. **Universal adult franchise** means that every adult citizen gets one vote and all votes are of equal value. To vote in India, a citizen must:
   - Be **18 years of age or above**.
   - Be **registered as a voter** in their constituency.
   (A citizen may also be barred from voting if found guilty of certain serious crimes.)

7. A **direct election** is one where citizens directly vote to choose their representatives — for example, elections to the **Lok Sabha and State Legislative Assemblies**, or the Class Representative election at Suryodaya School, where students directly voted for Ahmed, Gurmat, or Ravi.
   An **indirect election** is one where representatives are chosen by other elected representatives rather than by citizens directly — for example, elections to the **Rajya Sabha**, where members are elected by the elected members (MLAs) of state legislative assemblies.

8. Any two of the following:
   - The party in government must not use government resources for election purposes; for example, **no new schemes or projects** may be announced that could influence voters.
   - Candidates are expected to exercise **discretion and restraint** to ensure elections are conducted peacefully.
   - **Influencing voters through gifts** in exchange for votes is punishable.

9. In the ECI's district-level structure, the **District Election Officer** oversees two key officials:
   - The **Returning Officer (RO)**, who **conducts the elections** in the district.
   - The **Electoral Registration Officer (ERO)**, who **manages the voter list** for the constituency.
   Together, they ensure that elections at the ground level are conducted smoothly and that voter records remain accurate.

10. In the Class Representative election at Suryodaya School, three candidates — Ahmed, Gurmat, and Ravi — contested, and Ms. Usha was appointed as the Election Officer to ensure fairness. On election day, students cast their votes using a **secret ballot**, marking an 'X' next to their preferred candidate. Out of 33 votes cast, one ballot was unmarked and declared invalid. The final count was:
    - Ahmed: 8 votes
    - Gurmat: 12 votes
    - Ravi: 10 votes

    **Gurmat, having received the highest number of votes, was declared the winner and became the new Class Representative** — even though her 12 votes were **less than half** of the 33 votes cast (and less than half of the 32 valid votes). This is exactly how the **First-Past-the-Post (FPTP)** system, which India uses for Lok Sabha and State Assembly elections, works: the candidate with the **most** votes wins the seat, and there is **no requirement to cross the 50% mark**. This is why, in real elections too, a winning candidate or party can come to power without securing a majority of the total votes cast in their constituency.

11. The **President of India** is not directly elected by citizens. Instead, an **Electoral College** is formed to elect the President, consisting of:
    - **Members of Parliament (MPs)** from both Houses of Parliament — the **Lok Sabha** (Lower House) and the **Rajya Sabha** (Upper House).
    - **Members of the Legislative Assemblies (MLAs)** from each of India's states, as well as from the Union Territories of **Delhi and Puducherry**.

    The voting method used is the **single transferable vote system**. Since the President represents the whole country, this method is designed to ensure the support of both the central and state governments, with more populous states having a greater say in the outcome.

    The following groups are **excluded** from this Electoral College (any two):
    - Nominated members of the Rajya Sabha (12 members).
    - Nominated members of State Legislative Assemblies.
    - Members of Legislative Councils (both elected and nominated) in states with **bicameral legislatures**.
    - Nominated members of the union territories of Delhi and Puducherry.

    These groups are excluded because the election is designed so that only **directly elected representatives** of the people take part, ensuring that the President's election maintains democratic legitimacy and reflects, even if indirectly, the will of the people.
