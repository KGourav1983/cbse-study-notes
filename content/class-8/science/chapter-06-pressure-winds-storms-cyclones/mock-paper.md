12. Solve the following: A force of 20000 N acts on the base of an elephant's four feet, where the area of each foot is 0.25 m². (a) Calculate the total area covered by all four feet. ## Answer Key

1. **Pressure** is defined as the force per unit area. Pressure = Force / Area.

2. The SI unit of pressure is **newton/metre² (N/m²)**, also called a **pascal (Pa)**.

3. **Atmospheric pressure** is the pressure exerted by the air around us.

4. A **thunderstorm** is a storm accompanied by lightning and thunder.

5. The **eye of the cyclone** is the region of lowest pressure at the centre of a cyclone, where the wind is calm, while the surrounding region experiences strong winds and heavy rainfall.

6. The Amphan cyclone (2020) had peak wind speeds of **270 km/h**.

7. The pointed end of a nail has a much **smaller area** compared to its flat head. Since pressure is force per unit area, applying the same force over this smaller area produces a **much higher pressure**, which makes it easier for the nail to penetrate the wood. Driving the nail with the flat head instead would spread the same force over a larger area, producing a much lower pressure, making it harder to drive in.

8. Any two of the following: stay away from tall objects; find a low-lying open area and crouch down (do not lie flat); minimise contact with the ground; avoid using an umbrella with a metallic rod; get out of water if you are in it; stay inside a bus or car, which is comparatively safer.

9. The pressure exerted by a liquid **depends on the height of its column** — a greater height produces greater pressure. By placing an overhead water tank at a height, the pressure in the taps below is **increased**, resulting in a stronger, more powerful stream of water from the taps.

10. Water stored in a dam exerts pressure **horizontally on the side walls**, and this horizontal pressure is **very large near the bottom** of the dam, since pressure increases with the height of the water column above that point. To withstand this large pressure near the bottom and support the structure of the dam, the **base is made much broader** than the top.

11. When high-speed winds blow **over** the roof of a house, a **low-pressure area is created above the roof**, since high-speed winds are accompanied by a reduced pressure. If the doors and windows remain closed, there is a large pressure difference between the air inside the house (higher pressure) and the air above the roof (lower pressure), and if this difference is large enough, the roof may be **blown away**. Keeping the doors and windows open allows wind to also pass **through** the house, which **reduces the pressure difference** between the inside of the house and the region above the roof, helping to **prevent the roof from being blown off**.

12. (a) Total area covered by all four feet = 4 × 0.25 m² = **1 m²**

    (b) Using the formula:
    $$\text{Pressure} = \frac{\text{Force}}{\text{Area}} = \frac{20000\text{ N}}{1\text{ m}^2} = 20000\text{ N/m}^2 \text{ (20000 Pa)}$$

    (c) Animals with a **larger foot area**, like an elephant, spread their body weight (force) over a **larger surface area**. Since pressure is force per unit area, a larger area results in a **lower pressure** exerted on the ground for the same amount of force/weight. This lower pressure means the ground beneath each foot experiences less concentrated force, making it **less likely for the animal to sink into soft ground**. In contrast, an animal of similar weight but with a smaller foot area would exert the same force over a much smaller area, resulting in much higher pressure and a greater tendency to sink into soft or loose ground.

13. **Formation of a cyclone (step-by-step):**
    - Cyclones are large storms that form over **warm ocean waters**. As the ocean water gets heated, the **warm and moist air above it rises**.
    - As this moist air rises, the water vapour in it **condenses to form raindrops**. Since water takes up heat to change into vapour during evaporation, when this vapour condenses back into raindrops, that **heat is released back into the atmosphere**.
    - This released heat causes **further warming of the ascending air**, leading it to rise even further, which creates an **even lower pressure** area at that location.
    - Air from the **surrounding regions rushes in** to fill this low-pressure zone, and this air also starts rising in turn.
    - **Earth's rotation causes the moving air to spin.**
    - This entire cycle repeats itself, resulting in the creation of a **very low-pressure area with high-speed winds revolving around it**. This spinning system of clouds, winds, and rain is called a **cyclone**.
    - At the **centre** of the cyclone lies the region of **lowest pressure**, called the **eye of the cyclone**, where the wind is calm, while the surrounding region experiences strong winds and heavy rainfall.
    - As the cyclone moves from the ocean towards land, it generates **higher wind speeds** compared to regular thunderstorms. Once it reaches land, its **source of moist air is cut off**, and it **gradually loses strength**.

    **Destructive effects once a cyclone reaches land:**
    - Strong winds push ocean water towards the shore, creating a **wall of water as high as 3-12 metres**, which can **flood coastal areas** and even areas far from the sea.
    - The **heavy rainfall** accompanying the cyclone can cause **rivers to overflow** and may also **trigger landslides**.
    - **Seawater rushing inland can contaminate drinking water sources** and damage farmland, since the **salt in seawater makes soil less fertile**, affecting crops.
    - **Roads may get blocked** by fallen trees and debris, making it difficult for relief and rescue efforts to reach the affected areas.
    - **Power outages can last for days**, disrupting emergency services and daily life for the affected population.

    Even as a cyclone loses strength while moving over land, the destruction it leaves behind can take **months or even years** to repair, making cyclones one of the most extremely destructive natural events.
