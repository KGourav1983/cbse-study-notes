### Q1: Define pressure and state its formula.
**Pressure** is defined as the **force per unit area**. It is calculated using the formula: Pressure = Force / Area.

### Q2: State the SI unit of pressure.
The SI unit of pressure is **newton/metre² (N/m²)**, also called a **pascal**, denoted by **Pa**.

### Q3: What is atmospheric pressure?
The pressure exerted by the **air around us** is known as **atmospheric pressure**.

### Q4: What is a storm?
The **strong winds accompanied by rain** is called a **storm**.

### Q5: What is a thunderstorm?
A **storm accompanied by lightning and thunder** is called a **thunderstorm**.

### Q6: What is a cyclone?
A **cyclone** is a spinning system of clouds, winds, and rain formed when warm, moist air rises over warm ocean waters, creating a very low-pressure area with high-speed winds revolving around it, due to the effect of Earth's rotation.

### Q7: What is the eye of the cyclone?
The **eye of the cyclone** is the region of **lowest pressure** at the centre of a cyclone, where the wind is calm, even though the surrounding region experiences strong winds and heavy rainfall.

### Q8: Why is it easier to cut with the sharp edge of a knife than the blunt edge?
The sharp edge of a knife has a much **smaller area** than the blunt edge. Since pressure is force per unit area, applying the same force over a smaller area produces a **much higher pressure**, making it easier to cut through the object.

### Q9: Explain why liquids stored in a container, such as water in a bottle, exert pressure on the sides of the container.
When holes are made at the same height around the sides of a water-filled bottle, water spurts out from all the holes, showing that the liquid pushes outward on the container walls. This demonstrates that **liquids exert pressure not only at the bottom but also on the sides of a container**, i.e., **liquids exert pressure in all directions**.

### Q10: Why are overhead water tanks placed at a height?
The pressure exerted by a liquid **depends on the height of its column** — a taller water column produces greater pressure. Placing an overhead tank at a height **increases the pressure in the taps below it**, resulting in a stronger, more powerful stream of water from the taps.

### Q11: Explain why we do not get crushed by the very high atmospheric pressure acting on our bodies.
Although the atmospheric air column exerts a very large force on our bodies (nearly equal to the weight of a 225 kg object over a 15 cm × 15 cm area), we are not crushed because the **pressure inside our bodies is also equal to the atmospheric pressure**. This internal pressure, caused by the movement of fluids and gases in our tissues and organs, **balances** the pressure exerted from outside.

### Q12: State the conclusion drawn from the activity in which two balloons are connected by a straw, one inflated and one uninflated.
Air flows from the **inflated balloon (higher pressure)** to the **uninflated balloon (lower pressure)** through the straw, causing changes in the size of both balloons. This flow continues until the pressure in both balloons becomes equal, at which point both balloons reach nearly the same size and the airflow stops. This demonstrates that **air moves from a region of high air pressure to a region of low air pressure**.

### Q13: Why is it safer to keep doors and windows open during a storm with high-speed winds?
High-speed winds blowing over a house's roof create a **low-pressure area above the roof**, while the pressure inside/below the roof remains comparatively higher. If this pressure difference is large and the roof is weak, it may be **blown away**. Keeping doors and windows open allows the wind to also pass **through** the house, which **reduces the pressure difference** between the inside of the house and the region above the roof, helping to **prevent the roof from being blown off**.

### Q14: Explain, with reference to the activity involving two balloons hung close together, how blowing air between them demonstrates the relationship between wind speed and air pressure.
When air is blown into the narrow gap between two hanging balloons, the balloons **move towards each other** instead of apart. This happens because blowing air between the balloons **creates a low-pressure area** in the gap, and the **higher air pressure surrounding** the balloons pushes them towards each other. Blowing harder increases the speed at which the balloons approach each other, demonstrating that **high-speed winds are accompanied by a reduced (lower) air pressure**.

### Q15: Explain the formation of sea breeze and land breeze based on the concept of air pressure.
During the **day**, land heats up faster than water, so the warm, lighter air above the land **rises**, creating a **low-pressure area** over the land. Air from the **high-pressure region over the sea** then flows towards this low-pressure land area, resulting in a **sea breeze**. At **night**, water remains warmer than land, so a **low-pressure area develops above the sea** instead, causing wind to blow from the land to the sea, resulting in a **land breeze**. Thus, both phenomena are mainly due to **pressure differences between the land and the sea**.

### Q16: Explain the process by which a thunderstorm is formed.
When land gets heated, warm and moist air, being lighter, **rises**, creating a low-pressure area, and cooler air from the surrounding high-pressure areas flows in to take its place, creating a continuous cycle of wind circulation. As the rising air **expands, it cools**, and moisture in it **condenses** to form water droplets, creating clouds. These droplets merge to form heavier drops which fall as **rain, hail, or snow**. Strong winds blowing **upwards and downwards** within the cloud cause water droplets and ice particles to rub against each other, generating **electric charges**. A storm accompanied by the resulting **lightning and thunder** is called a **thunderstorm**.

### Q17: Explain the process that leads to the formation of lightning.
Strong winds blowing upwards and downwards within storm clouds cause **water droplets and ice particles to rub against each other**, generating static electric charges. The **positively charged, lighter ice particles** move upward and occupy the **upper part** of the cloud, while the **negatively charged, heavier water droplets** occupy the **lower part**, creating a **charge separation** within the cloud. Normally, air acts as an **electrical insulator**, keeping opposite charges from meeting. However, when the charge build-up becomes very large, the insulating property of air **breaks down**, and a **sudden flow of charges** takes place, producing a bright flash of light called **lightning**. Lightning can occur within a cloud, between clouds, or between a cloud and the ground.

### Q18: List any four precautions that should be taken to stay safe during lightning.
Any four of the following:
- **Stay away from tall objects**
- Find a **low-lying open area** and **crouch down**; do not lie down flat
- **Minimise contact with the ground**
- **Avoid using an umbrella with a metallic rod**
- If in water, **get out of it**
- Being inside a **bus or car** is comparatively safer

### Q19: What is a lightning conductor, and how does it protect a building?
A **lightning conductor** is a metallic rod installed along the walls of a building during construction. One end of the rod is **pointed** and kept **higher than the highest point of the building**, while the other end is **buried deep in the ground**. The rod provides an **easy path for the transfer of electric charges into the ground**, protecting the building from the destructive effects of a lightning strike.

### Q20: Explain, step by step, how a cyclone forms over warm ocean waters.
As ocean water gets heated, the warm and moist air above it **rises**. As this moist air rises, water vapour **condenses to form raindrops**, and since condensation releases the heat that was absorbed during evaporation, this released heat causes **further warming of the rising air**, making it rise even more and creating an **even lower pressure** area. Air from the surrounding regions **rushes in** to fill this low-pressure zone and also starts rising. **Earth's rotation causes this moving air to spin**, and this cycle repeats, resulting in the creation of a very **low-pressure area with high-speed winds revolving around it** — this spinning system of clouds, winds, and rain is called a **cyclone**.

### Q21: Explain why the base of a dam is made much broader than its top.
Water stored in a dam exerts pressure **horizontally on the side walls** and **vertically on the floor**, due to the height of the water level. Since the horizontal water pressure is **very large near the bottom** of the dam (pressure increases with the height/depth of the liquid column), the base of the dam is made **broader** so that it can support the structure and **withstand this large horizontal pressure** near the bottom.

### Q22: Describe an activity that shows air exerts pressure, and explain the observation and conclusion.
An inverted paper plate with a stick attached is first covered with a **folded** sheet of chart paper and lifted using the stick, noting the effort required. It is then covered with the **same sheet unfolded** (same weight, but a larger area) and lifted again. It is observed that **more effort is needed** to lift the paper plate when covered with the **unfolded** (larger area) sheet, even though the weight of the covering sheet has **not changed**. This shows that **air exerts a force on the covering sheet**, and this force **increases as the area of the sheet increases**. Since force per unit area is pressure, we conclude that **air exerts pressure**, and in fact, air exerts pressure on all objects around us.

### Q23: A cyclone loses its strength once it reaches land, but the destruction it causes can take months or years to repair. Explain how a cyclone causes destruction, listing at least four ways.
Any four of the following, with explanation:
- **Strong winds push ocean water toward the shore**, creating a wall of water as high as 3-12 metres, which can **flood coastal areas** and even areas far from the sea.
- The **heavy rainfall** accompanying a cyclone may cause **rivers to overflow** and can also **trigger landslides**.
- **Seawater that rushes inland can contaminate drinking water sources** and damage farmland — the **salt in seawater makes soil less fertile**, affecting crops.
- **Roads may get blocked** due to fallen trees and debris, making it difficult for rescue and relief to reach affected areas.
- **Power outages can last for days**, disrupting emergency services and daily life.

### Q24: An elephant stands on four feet. If the area covered by one foot is 0.25 m², calculate the pressure exerted by the elephant on the ground if its weight is 20000 N.
Total area covered by all four feet = 4 × 0.25 m² = **1 m²**

Using the formula:
$$\text{Pressure} = \frac{\text{Force}}{\text{Area}} = \frac{20000\text{ N}}{1\text{ m}^2}$$

$$\text{Pressure} = 20000\text{ N/m}^2 \text{ (or 20000 Pa)}$$

The pressure exerted by the elephant on the ground is **20000 N/m² (20000 Pa)**.

### Q25: Two boats, A and B, are compared: Boat A has a base area of 7 m² and carries 5 persons; Boat B has a base area of 3.5 m² and carries 3 persons. If each person weighs 700 N, find out which boat experiences more pressure on its base, and by how much.
**For Boat A:**
Total force (weight of 5 persons) = 5 × 700 N = 3500 N
$$\text{Pressure}_A = \frac{\text{Force}}{\text{Area}} = \frac{3500\text{ N}}{7\text{ m}^2} = 500\text{ N/m}^2$$

**For Boat B:**
Total force (weight of 3 persons) = 3 × 700 N = 2100 N
$$\text{Pressure}_B = \frac{\text{Force}}{\text{Area}} = \frac{2100\text{ N}}{3.5\text{ m}^2} = 600\text{ N/m}^2$$

**Comparison:**
Since Pressure$_B$ (600 N/m²) > Pressure$_A$ (500 N/m²), **Boat B experiences more pressure** on its base.

**Difference in pressure** = 600 N/m² − 500 N/m² = **100 N/m² (100 Pa)**

Therefore, **Boat B experiences more pressure than Boat A, by 100 N/m² (100 Pa)**.
