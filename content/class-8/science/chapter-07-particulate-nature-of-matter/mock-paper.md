## Answer Key

1. A **constituent particle** is the basic unit that makes up a larger piece of a substance or material.

2. The melting point of ice is **0 °C**.

3. **Acharya Kanad** first proposed the idea of the *Parmanu* (atom).

4. **False.** Gases have neither a fixed shape nor a fixed volume; they occupy all the available space of their container.

5. Liquids and gases are together called **fluids**, because both of them flow and do not have a fixed shape.

6. Grinding a stick of chalk is a **physical change** because the chalk does not turn into a new substance — only the size of each speck of chalk is reduced. Each tiny grain of the resulting powder is still made of the same chalk, showing that no new substance has been formed, which is the defining feature of a physical change (as opposed to a chemical change).

7. 
   - **Surface vs. whole liquid:** Evaporation occurs slowly and only at the **surface** of the liquid, while boiling occurs rapidly and throughout the **whole liquid**, seen as bubble formation.
   - **Temperature:** Evaporation can occur at **any temperature**, whereas boiling occurs only at a fixed temperature called the **boiling point**, at which vapour formation becomes very fast.

8. Gases can be compressed easily because their particles have a **large amount of space** between them in their natural state, and this space can be reduced by applying external pressure, forcing the particles closer together. Liquids, on the other hand, have particles that are **already closely packed**, with very little interparticle space remaining, so applying pressure cannot bring them much closer — making liquids **practically incompressible**.

9. When sugar is placed in water, **water particles are able to pull the constituent particles of sugar out of its grain** and spread them into the interparticle spaces of the water, allowing sugar to dissolve. In sand, however, the **constituent particles are held together by attraction too strong** for the water particles to overcome and pull apart, so sand remains undissolved and settles at the bottom of the container, making it insoluble in water.

10. 
    - **Solids:** Particles are **closely/tightly packed** with **minimum interparticle spacing**. Interparticle attraction is **maximum**, restricting particle movement to only small vibrations about fixed positions.
    - **Liquids:** Particles are **a little more loosely packed** than in solids, with slightly larger interparticle spacing. Interparticle attraction is **slightly weaker than in solids**, allowing particles to move, but only within a limited space.
    - **Gases:** Particles have **maximum interparticle spacing** and are essentially **free** to move. Interparticle attraction is **negligible**, so particles move rapidly in all available directions.
    
    (A correctly labelled diagram should show: solid particles in a tightly packed, ordered arrangement; liquid particles slightly more spread out and less ordered; gas particles widely and randomly scattered with large gaps between them.)

11. Initially, when sugar is added to water (before stirring), the water level rises because the undissolved sugar grains occupy space in addition to the water. However, once the sugar is stirred and fully dissolved, the water level drops slightly compared to that mark, because the volume of the resulting solution is **less than the sum of the individual volumes of water and sugar**. This happens because the dissolved sugar's constituent particles do not simply add on top of the water's volume — instead, they slot into the **empty interparticle spaces** that already exist between the water particles. This proves that even in a liquid like water, there is measurable space between the constituent particles.

12. **Observations:** When a few grains of potassium permanganate are dropped into water without stirring, streaks of pink colour are initially seen spreading out from the grain. With time, the entire bulk of water acquires a uniform pink colour.
    **Explanation of temperature effect:** This spreading (diffusion) happens because water particles are in **constant motion**, pulling potassium permanganate particles from the grain and carrying them through the liquid via collisions. In **hot water**, particles possess more thermal energy and therefore move faster and more vigorously, so the pink colour spreads **fastest**. In **room-temperature water**, the spread is moderate, and in **ice-cold water**, particles have the least thermal energy and move the slowest, so the colour spreads **most slowly**. This confirms that the **movement of particles increases as heat/thermal energy is provided**.

13. The fragrance particles released by the burning incense stick are constantly struck by the **rapidly and freely moving air particles** in the room. Since gas particles have negligible interparticle attraction and move in all available directions, these collisions push the fragrance particles further and further from the source. Over a few minutes, this constant particle movement carries the fragrance particles throughout the entire room, which is why the smell — initially noticeable only near the incense stick — eventually reaches every corner of the room.
