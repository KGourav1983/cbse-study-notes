## Answer Key

1. A **mixture** is formed when two or more substances are mixed together such that each substance retains its own properties, and the components do not react chemically with each other.

2. **Alloys** are uniform mixtures of two or more metals, mixed so uniformly that individual substances cannot be seen. Example: **stainless steel** (iron, nickel, chromium, and a small amount of carbon) — or brass (copper + zinc) or bronze (copper + tin).

3. A **native mineral** is a mineral that exists as a pure element (not a compound) — for example, metals like gold, silver, copper, or non-metals like sulfur and carbon.

4. **False.** A pure substance cannot be separated into other kinds of matter by any physical process — this is exactly what defines it as pure.

5. **Hydrogen gas** produces a 'pop' sound when tested with a burning candle.

6. 
   - **Uniform mixtures**: components are evenly distributed and cannot be distinguished, even with a microscope. Example: sugar dissolved in water.
   - **Non-uniform mixtures**: components are generally visible with the naked eye or a magnifying device. Example: a sprout salad containing visible green gram, chickpeas, onion, and tomato.

7. The ratio of hydrogen to oxygen atoms in water is **2:1**. On electrolysis (passing electricity through water containing a little dilute sulfuric acid), water breaks down as:
   **Water → Hydrogen + Oxygen**

8. When sugar is heated in a boiling tube, it turns brown and then chars to black, leaving behind **charcoal (carbon)**, with water droplets forming near the mouth of the tube (coming from the sugar itself, since the tube is being heated, not cooled). Since sugar decomposes into **carbon and water** on heating, and water itself is made of the elements hydrogen and oxygen, sugar must be made of more than one element. Therefore, sugar is a **compound** consisting of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen, and not an element.

9. The word equation is:
   **Iron + Dilute Hydrochloric acid → Iron chloride + Hydrogen gas**
   The hydrogen gas produced is **colourless and has no smell**, and it burns with a characteristic **'pop' sound** when tested with a burning splinter or candle.

10. 

    | Test | Sample A (Mixture of iron + sulfur) | Sample B (Iron sulfide, heated) |
    |---|---|---|
    | **Appearance** | Black and yellow coloured particles visible separately | Uniform black colour and texture throughout |
    | **Magnet test** | Iron filings get attracted to the magnet; iron and sulfur can be separated | No effect of the magnet; components cannot be separated |
    | **Reaction with dilute HCl** | Iron reacts to form iron chloride and colourless, odourless **hydrogen gas** (burns with a 'pop' sound); sulfur is left unreacted as a yellow solid | Iron sulfide reacts to form iron chloride and colourless **hydrogen sulfide gas**, which has a rotten egg-like odour |

    This clearly shows Sample A is a **mixture** (components retain individual properties and are separable), while Sample B is a **compound** (a new substance with entirely different, non-separable properties).

11. This statement is justified because compounds always possess properties **completely different** from their constituent elements. **Hydrogen** is a highly flammable fuel, and **oxygen** actively supports combustion — logically, one might expect their combination to be dangerously flammable. However, water, formed when hydrogen and oxygen combine chemically in a fixed 2:1 ratio, does the exact opposite: it **extinguishes fire** rather than burning or aiding combustion. This dramatic contrast between the properties of the elements and the compound they form illustrates the defining feature of a compound: **new properties emerge that are distinct from those of its constituent elements**, and this new substance cannot be separated back into hydrogen and oxygen by any physical method — only by a chemical process like electrolysis.

12. Take a glass tumbler, fill it half with water, and add a small amount of calcium oxide (quicklime) slowly. Calcium oxide reacts vigorously with water, releasing heat and forming calcium hydroxide. On stirring, filtering, and collecting the colourless solution (called **lime water**) in a petri dish, and leaving it exposed to air for a few hours with periodic stirring, the lime water gradually **turns milky**. This happens because carbon dioxide present in the air reacts with the calcium hydroxide in the lime water to form calcium carbonate (insoluble tiny white particles) and water:
    **Calcium hydroxide + Carbon dioxide → Calcium carbonate + Water**
    Since the lime water turns milky only when exposed to air, this activity confirms the presence of carbon dioxide in the air.

13. 
    - **Gold**: an **element** — it is made of identical gold atoms and cannot be broken down into simpler substances; it is also a native mineral found in its pure elemental form.
    - **Sodium chloride**: a **compound** — formed when the elements sodium (a soft metal) and chlorine (a hazardous gas) combine chemically in a fixed 1:1 ratio to form a completely new, safe substance.
    - **Seawater**: a **mixture** — a uniform mixture in which dissolved salts and other substances are evenly distributed in water but retain their individual chemical identities.
    - **Oxygen**: an **element** — made of identical oxygen atoms/molecules, one of the two gases obtained when water undergoes electrolysis.
    - **Iron sulfide**: a **compound** — formed by the chemical combination of iron and sulfur in a fixed ratio on heating; it has properties (such as non-magnetism and reaction with HCl to give hydrogen sulfide) completely different from its constituent elements.
    - **Air**: a **mixture** — a uniform mixture mainly of nitrogen, oxygen, argon, carbon dioxide, and water vapour, along with suspended dust particles as pollutants.
