## Answer Key

1. The **root** symbol represents the deep, solid foundation of knowledge that keeps us connected to our environment, traditions, and cultural and natural heritage.

2. Any one of the following: whether the puri **puffs up** (yes/no), the **time it takes to puff up** (in seconds), or whether a very thick layer of dough still gives a thin side to the puri.

3. **Systematic investigation** is the step-by-step process used in all scientific experiments, where a scientific question is asked, variables to control and observe are identified, one variable is changed at a time while keeping others constant, observations are carefully noted, and results lead to further questions.

4. The two effects of electric current mentioned are the **heating effect** and the **magnetic effect**.

5. Earth lies at the **perfect distance from the Sun**, which allows water to remain in its liquid state.

6. In **Grade 6**, students discovered that science begins with **wonder** — with simple "Why?" and "How?" questions about the world around them. In **Grade 7**, students learnt that science is always **evolving** — that each answer opens new questions, and that ideas can slowly change as one explores deeper. Grade 8 builds on both of these by introducing **investigation** — asking focused questions and designing experiments to answer them.

7. Any two of the following: the **thickness and size** of the rolled dough, the **type of flour** used (atta, maida, etc.), the **temperature of the hot oil**, or the **way the dough is dropped** into the oil (vertically, at an angle, or slowly).

8. It is important to change only **one variable at a time**, keeping all other conditions the same, so that any difference observed in the result can be clearly attributed to that single changed variable. For example, to test the effect of oil temperature on puffing, one must use dough circles of the **same thickness** and drop them **in the same way** each time — otherwise, it would not be possible to tell whether the oil temperature, or some other factor, caused the difference in the outcome.

9. An **element** is a pure substance. A **compound** is formed when two or more elements are **bonded together**. A **mixture** is a combination of substances that **can be separated physically**.

10. The phases of the Moon arise because, depending on the relative positions of the **Earth, Moon, and Sun**, a slightly different part of the Moon is **illuminated** each night. By watching these periodic cycles of the Moon's phases, along with observations of sunrises and sunsets, early humans were able to develop the **first calendars** — showing that the calendars which determine our daily routines on Earth are linked to the motions of objects far beyond our planet.
