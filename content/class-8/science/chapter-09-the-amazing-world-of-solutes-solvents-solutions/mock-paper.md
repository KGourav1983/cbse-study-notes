## Answer Key

1. **Solubility** is the maximum amount of solute that can be dissolved in a fixed quantity of solvent (or solution) at a particular temperature.

2. The SI unit of density is **kilogram per cubic metre (kg/m³)**.

3. **Saturated**.

4. **False.** Oxygen gas is more soluble in **cold water** rather than hot water — the solubility of gases generally decreases as temperature increases.

5. A **balance** (e.g., a digital weighing balance) is used to measure the mass of an object.

6. A **saturated solution** is one in which the maximum amount of solute has already dissolved at a given temperature, and no more solute can dissolve — any additional solute added settles at the bottom. An **unsaturated solution** is one in which more solute can still be dissolved at that same temperature. In other words, a saturated solution has reached its solubility limit, while an unsaturated solution has not.

7. Using the density formula:
   $$\text{Density} = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Volume}} = \frac{400\ \text{g}}{40\ \text{cm}^3} = 10\ \text{g/cm}^3$$
   The density of the object is **10 g/cm³**.

8. Measuring cylinders are designed narrow and tall so that a given change in volume corresponds to a **larger, more visible change in the height of the liquid column**, allowing for **finer, more accurate readings** on the scale. If a container were wide and short (like a beaker), the same volume of liquid would spread out over a much larger cross-sectional area, causing only a small change in height for a given volume — making it much harder to read small volume differences accurately.

9. As air is heated, its particles gain energy and **move apart**, causing the volume of the air to increase while its **mass remains unchanged**. Since Density = Mass/Volume, this increase in volume with constant mass causes the **density of the hot air to decrease**, making it less dense than the surrounding cooler air. Because less dense substances rise above denser ones, the hot air moves upward — this is the same principle used in **hot air balloons**.

10. **Step 1: Find the volume of the stone using the displacement method.**
    $$\text{Volume of stone} = \text{Final volume} - \text{Initial volume} = 55\ \text{mL} - 50\ \text{mL} = 5\ \text{mL} = 5\ \text{cm}^3$$
    **Step 2: Apply the density formula.**
    $$\text{Density} = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Volume}} = \frac{16.400\ \text{g}}{5\ \text{cm}^3} = 3.28\ \text{g/cm}^3$$
    The density of the stone is **3.28 g/cm³**.

11. When a solid is dissolved in a liquid to form a solution, the **solid component is always called the solute**, and the **liquid component is always called the solvent**, regardless of their relative quantities — this rule applies only when mixing two liquids (where the substance in smaller quantity is called the solute). This is clearly illustrated by the ***Chashni*** (sugar syrup) used to prepare Gulab jamun: it is made of a **large amount of sugar (solid)** dissolved in a **small amount of water (liquid)**. Even though sugar is present in a much greater quantity than water, water is still considered the **solvent**, and sugar is still considered the **solute**, because the solid-in-liquid classification takes priority over the "larger vs smaller amount" rule used for liquid-liquid mixtures.

12. Water has a special property: its **density is highest at 4 °C**. As the temperature of water drops further and it freezes into ice at 0 °C, the particles of water undergo a change in structure and rearrange themselves in a way that takes up **more space** — this process is called **expansion**. Since the same mass of water now occupies a **larger volume** as ice, and Density = Mass/Volume, the **density of ice becomes lower** than that of liquid water. Because ice is **less dense (lighter)** than liquid water, it **floats** on the surface rather than sinking. This is important for aquatic ecosystems because the floating ice forms an insulating layer on top of lakes and oceans, keeping the water underneath warm enough for fish and other creatures to survive even in extremely cold weather.

13. Rearranging the density formula (Density = Mass/Volume) to solve for volume:
    $$\text{Volume} = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Density}} = \frac{600\ \text{g}}{7.9\ \text{g/cm}^3} \approx 75.9\ \text{cm}^3$$
    The volume of the iron block is approximately **75.9 cm³**.
